# classical-and-deep-learning-based-image-denoising-on-brain-MRI-data
This project presents a complete pipeline for evaluating and comparing classical and deep learning-based image denoising techniques on brain MRI data.
The notebook investigates the robustness of different denoising approaches under multiple noise conditions commonly encountered in medical imaging.

## Noise Models
- Gaussian Noise
- Speckle Noise
- Salt & Pepper Noise
## Denoising Methods
- BM3D (classical method)
- DnCNN (deep convolutional neural network)
- SwinIR (transformer-based model)
## Evaluation Metrics

Performance is quantitatively assessed using:

- PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio)
- SSIM (Structural Similarity Index)
- MSE (Mean Squared Error)
## Our Goal

To provide a clear comparison between traditional and modern deep learning approaches for MRI denoising, highlighting their strengths and limitations across different noise scenarios.
